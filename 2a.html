Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, India, dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria. It was built between 1906 and 1921 and is now a popular tourist attraction in the city.

History

The Victoria Memorial was designed by William Emerson, a British architect, and was funded by Indian princes and British citizens. It was built to commemorate Queen Victoria's death in 1901 and to celebrate the British Empire's rule in India.

Architecture

The Victoria Memorial is built in the Indo-Saracenic Revivalist style, which blends elements of British and Indian architecture. The building is made of white Makrana marble and is surrounded by gardens and water bodies. The memorial has several galleries and exhibition spaces, which house a collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts related to the British Raj in India.

Visiting the Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is open to visitors from 10am to 5pm every day except Mondays and national holidays. Entry tickets can be purchased at the entrance gate, and audio guides are available for a small fee. Visitors are not allowed to carry cameras or bags inside the memorial, but there is a cloakroom where these items can be stored.
