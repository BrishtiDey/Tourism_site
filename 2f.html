Indian Museum Kolkata

The Indian Museum Kolkata is the oldest and largest museum in India. Located in the heart of Kolkata, it is a must-visit for anyone interested in the history, culture and art of India. The museum has a vast collection of over 102,000 exhibits, including art, anthropology, archaeology, geology, zoology, and botany.

History

The Indian Museum was established in 1814 by the Asiatic Society of Bengal, a learned society based in Kolkata. It was founded by Dr. Nathaniel Wallich, a Danish botanist, who was the curator of the society. The museum started with a collection of just a few hundred items, but over time it grew to become one of the largest and most prestigious museums in the world.

Collections

The Indian Museum has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits covering a wide range of subjects. The museum has galleries dedicated to art, anthropology, archaeology, geology, zoology, and botany. Some of the highlights of the collection include:

  • The fossil of the extinct dinosaur Rajasaurus Narmadensis
  • The world's largest collection of meteorites
  • The only surviving specimen of the Great Indian One-Horned Rhinoceros
  • A collection of ancient Indian coins and sculptures
  • Artifacts from the Indus Valley Civilization

Visiting the Indian Museum

The Indian Museum is open to visitors from Tuesday to Sunday from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM. The museum is closed on Mondays and on certain holidays. Visitors can purchase tickets at the entrance of the museum or online in advance. Guided tours are also available for an additional fee. Visitors are not allowed to take photographs inside the museum and are requested to maintain decorum while visiting.
