Fort William

Fort William is a large fort located in the heart of Kolkata, India. It was built by the British East India Company in the 18th century and named after King William III of England. The fort played an important role in the history of British India and is now a popular tourist attraction.

History

Fort William was built in 1781 by the British East India Company to protect the city of Kolkata from foreign invasions. The fort was named after King William III of England and was designed by Captain John Brohier, a British engineer. The fort was the site of several important events in the history of British India, including the infamous Black Hole of Calcutta incident in 1756, which resulted in the deaths of more than 100 British prisoners.

Architecture

Fort William is a large fort with an area of over 70 acres. The fort is surrounded by a moat and has several bastions and gateways. The fort also houses a large open space known as the Parade Ground, which is used for military drills and public events. The architecture of the fort is a mix of British and Indian styles, with elements such as red brick walls and arched gateways.

Visiting Fort William

Fort William is open to visitors and offers a glimpse into the history of British India. Visitors can explore the fort and see its various structures, including the Governor's House and the Military Museum. The fort also offers a light and sound show in the evenings, which tells the story of the fort's history. Visitors should be aware that the fort is still used by the Indian Army and certain areas may be restricted.
